Key Findings

📰 Press Scores Skew High

↑
Our press scores consistently run 10–15 pts above Metacritic on high-reviewed games (Balatro: 100% vs 90, Outer Wilds: 100% vs 85, Pentiment: 95% vs 86). Articles skew toward positive coverage; negative reviews are rare in the small sample we pull.
⚠️
Exception: Black Myth (58% vs 81) and Hell Is Us (54% vs 77) — our press is more negative than Metacritic. Likely pulling opinion/analysis pieces alongside review coverage, or post-launch coverage that diverged from launch-day reviews.

👥 Player Scores Skew Lower than Steam

↓
Our AI extracts nuanced critiques even from "positive" reviews. Steam's binary thumbs-up inflates the all-time %. Our player scores average ~15–20 pts below Steam all-time — which is arguably more diagnostic for developers.
🎯
Hell Is Us player score: 49% — well below Steam's 87%. Players are expressing mixed views in their text even while clicking "Recommend." This gap is the signal developers care about.

🟠 Our Scores vs Sprung Sonar

≈
Our combined scores align directionally with Sprung: Balatro (ours ~86% combined vs Sprung 88%), Citizen Sleeper (ours ~69% vs Sprung 75%). Good signal quality despite 2–4× fewer insights.
📦
Insight gap narrowed: From 4.6× (Feb baseline: 80 vs 370 for Balatro) to 3.3× now (112 vs 370). Still significant — Sprung uses YouTube transcripts and community boards we don't yet access.

🔴 Critical Data Gaps

🚫
Palworld: zero press insights. Early Access title with limited traditional review coverage. Press score shows N/A — we need to handle EA titles differently (community forums, Discord).
🚫
Reddit blocked on GitHub Actions (Azure IP range). Adding Reddit OAuth as a GitHub Secret would unlock ~30+ insights per game, closing the gap further. Currently all player sentiment = Steam only.
📺
YouTube disabled (YT_BLOCKED=True). Sprung's primary differentiator. Re-enabling with transcript scraping could add 100+ insights per game and close the gap to <2×.
